Today's Deep Dive: STARKs on Bitcoin: The Massive Engineering Lift Hiding Behind PACTs

349w
Source: PACTs: Protecting Your Bitcoin From a Quantum Sunset – Dan Robinson (Paradigm) · KB #24

While the PACT commitment protocol is elegantly simple—hash, salt, timestamp via OpenTimestamps—the verification side of the equation is anything but. Robinson is candid that actually redeeming a PACT in a post-sunset Bitcoin would require verifying STARK proofs (Scalable Transparent Arguments of Knowledge) within Bitcoin's consensus rules. This represents one of the most ambitious protocol engineering challenges Bitcoin has ever faced.

The consensus proof for a PACT redemption must verify a complete anchor chain: the hidden salt and BIP-322 control proof hash to the public PACT commitment; the OpenTimestamps attestation carries that commitment through Merkle operations into a calendar root; that root appears in an OP_RETURN output of a confirmed Bitcoin transaction; the transaction is included in a block via a Merkle proof; and the block is recognized as part of the active chain before the PACT cutoff. All of this must be validated inside a STARK circuit and then verified by every Bitcoin full node.

Bitcoin's scripting system was deliberately designed to be minimal and deterministic. Introducing STARK verification would require substantial new plumbing—new opcodes or a verification framework, careful gas-metering to prevent denial-of-service attacks, and rigorous testing against adversarial inputs. The cryptographic assumptions underlying STARKs (hash-based, no trusted setup) align well with Bitcoin's ethos, but the implementation complexity is orders of magnitude beyond anything in Bitcoin's current consensus code.

Robinson's proposal wisely decouples the timeline: holders create PACTs now using existing tools, while the hard engineering of STARK verification is deferred to a future soft fork—potentially years away. This mirrors Bitcoin's historical pattern of conservative, staged upgrades. But it also means that PACT holders are making a bet that the community will eventually muster the political will and technical capability to ship this verification layer.

The STARK verification challenge also intersects with broader debates about Bitcoin's programmability. If Bitcoin ever adds native zero-knowledge proof verification, PACTs would be just one of many applications. The quantum sunset scenario may ultimately be the forcing function that brings ZK verification to Bitcoin—turning a defensive measure against quantum attackers into a catalyst for the protocol's most significant capability upgrade in years.
