Today's Deep Dive: The Mushroom Talk: Why a16z Treats LPs as Partners

222w
Source: Marc Andreessen: Will a16z Go Public & Why Labour Displacement with AI is Wrong (20VC) · KB #13

When Andreessen and Ben Horowitz started a16z in 2009, a legendary VC gave them what they call "the mushroom talk" about LPs: "Put them in a cardboard box, put the lid on the box, put the box under the bed, and don't open it for two years."

Andreessen and Horowitz had the opposite instinct. Having run public companies for 15 years, they'd dealt with hedge fund managers who were actively shorting their stock. Compared to that, LPs seemed pleasant — "at least when you walk in the room, you know they're not short your stock."

This framing decision — treating LPs as partners rather than mushrooms — became a structural advantage. The best LPs understand venture's time horizons, risk profiles, and volatility. Given license and transparency, they become enablers rather than constraints. a16z's LPs "have given us license to do a tremendous number of things that have been very risky, of which some have worked and some haven't."

The mushroom model reflects a scarcity mindset: information is power, sharing it dilutes power, so hoard it. The partnership model reflects an abundance mindset: transparency builds trust, trust enables risk-taking, risk-taking enables returns. Andreessen is essentially arguing that the traditional VC practice of keeping LPs in the dark was a self-imposed constraint that limited the kind of bold, conviction-driven investing that produces outlier returns.
